Research

People live longer, healthier lives when they have access to things like housing, health care, nutritious food, and a stable income. In my reserach, I investigate why the public support or oppose government programs aimed at providing these necessities. I also examine how cognitive and political biases affect people's support for paying taxes to fund social programs. In other research, I examine how personal biases and systemic factors (e.g., racial, geographical, political) perpetuate inequities in health and care delivery.

I am also a member of the Research Council at the nonprofit organization, A Healthier Democracy, where I investigate how civic engagement within underserved populations affects health outcomes. Below is a list of my ongoing projects along with my published research.
